# ESI Triage Guidelines

Quick reference for the Emergency Severity Index (ESI) five-level triage system.

## Severity Levels

| Level | Name | Criteria | Target Response |
|-------|------|----------|-----------------|
| ESI-1 | Resuscitation | Immediate life-saving intervention required | Immediate |
| ESI-2 | Emergent | High-risk situation, confused/lethargic, severe pain/distress, or vital sign concern | < 10 minutes |
| ESI-3 | Urgent | Two or more resources needed | < 30 minutes |
| ESI-4 | Less Urgent | One resource needed | < 60 minutes |
| ESI-5 | Non-Urgent | No resources needed | < 120 minutes |

## Decision Algorithm

1. **Is the patient dying?** (requires immediate life-saving intervention)
   - Yes -> ESI-1
2. **Should the patient NOT wait?** (high-risk, altered mental status, severe pain >= 7/10)
   - Yes -> ESI-2
3. **How many resources will this patient need?**
   - Two or more -> ESI-3
   - One -> ESI-4
   - None -> ESI-5

## Red Flags (Upgrade to ESI-1 or ESI-2)

- Chest pain with diaphoresis, radiation, or hemodynamic instability
- Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of my life")
- Signs of stroke (facial droop, arm drift, speech difficulty)
- Anaphylaxis (airway compromise, hypotension, urticaria)
- Active hemorrhage with hemodynamic compromise
- Altered mental status (GCS < 14)
- Respiratory distress (SpO2 < 90%, accessory muscle use)
- Severe sepsis indicators (fever + tachycardia + hypotension)

## Resource Counting Guide

A "resource" is any of the following:
- Lab tests (blood work, cultures, urinalysis)
- ECG / imaging (X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound)
- IV fluids or IV medications
- Specialty consultation
- Procedures (laceration repair, splinting, catheterization)

**Not counted as resources:** history, physical exam, oral medications, prescriptions,
simple wound care, crutches, slings.
